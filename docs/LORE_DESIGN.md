# VEINS — Design & Lore Bible (Living Draft)

## 1. Core Pitch

**Veins** is a browser-based 2D top-down co-op roguelike (2–4 players) where:

> Players do not build individual loadouts.  
> They construct a shared “Circulatory Board” that behaves like a living system.

Each run is a 20–40 minute descent into a procedural biome-organism called **The Veins**, where:

- Builds = beliefs
- Bosses = doctrinal tests
- The world = interpretive system
- The Heart = reality engine that responds to collective interpretation

---

## 2. Core Fantasy

Players are not heroes.

They are:

> **Vessels (Descendants of humanity)**

Awakening inside a collapsing cosmic-biological structure.

They descend not for power, but because:

> Reality is destabilizing and the “Pulse” is fading.

---

## 3. Central Innovation — Circulatory Board

### Concept

A shared hex-based relic board between all players.

Relics only reach full potential when:

- correctly positioned
- adjacent to compatible relics
- aligned with teammate relic structures

### Core Idea

> The party is a single organism, not separate characters.

### Consequences

- Buildcraft becomes collective
- Communication is mandatory
- Roles emerge dynamically
- “Solo optimization” is impossible

---

## 4. Core System Philosophy

### Implications

- Players express beliefs through builds (Doctrine)
- Bosses test those beliefs under pressure
- The Heart reacts to consistent collective behavior
- The world “interprets” player actions over time

---

## 5. The World — The Veins

### Definition

The Veins are:

> A living interpretive structure beneath reality.

They function as:

- biological system
- reality engine
- symbolic cosmology

### Bleed Clock

A global pressure system representing:

> Interpretive instability of reality

As players descend:
- Bleed Clock accelerates
- rewards increase
- stability decreases

---

## 6. The Heart (Core Entity)

### True Nature

The Heart is NOT a boss.

It is:

> A reality interpretation engine

It does not create reality.

It selects which interpretation becomes real.

### Implication

Reality is unstable because:

- multiple belief systems exist simultaneously
- factions impose conflicting truths
- interpretations compete for dominance

---

## 7. Factions / Doctrines

Players unconsciously align with doctrines based on playstyle.

### 🟦 Sanctum (Stability / Order)

- Controlled positioning
- Predictable synergy
- Minimal mutation
- Balanced playstyle

> “Truth is stable form.”

---

### 🟥 Tumorous Host (Mutation / Chaos)

- Adaptive builds
- High-risk high-reward
- Constant evolution
- Aggressive improvisation

> “Truth is constant becoming.”

---

### 🟩 Synaptic Chorus (Unity / Synchronization)

- Heavy coordination
- Shared effects
- Team dependency
- Perfect synergy scaling

> “Truth is collective mind.”

---

### ⬛ Penitent (Sacrifice / Withdrawal)

- Resource sacrifice
- Hesitation-aware gameplay
- Defensive pacing
- Loss-driven scaling

> “Truth is surrender.”

---

## 8. Relic System (Doctrine Vocabulary)

Relics are not items.

They are:

> Arguments about how reality works.

Each relic belongs loosely to a doctrine but can mix.

### Examples

#### Sanctum Relics
- Isolation bonuses
- stable adjacency rules
- predictable scaling

#### Chorus Relics
- shared cooldowns
- linked vision
- synchronized effects

#### Tumor Relics
- mutation mechanics
- adaptive scaling
- unpredictable transformation

#### Penitent Relics
- sacrifice conversion
- death-based scaling
- restraint bonuses

---

## 9. Boss System — Doctrine Tests

Bosses are not enemies.

They are:

> Externalized ideological contradictions.

### Core Mechanic

Each boss:
- embodies a doctrine
- tests opposing player behavior
- becomes easier if players align with or resist it coherently

---

### Example Boss Archetypes

#### Sanctum Boss
- punishes chaos
- rewards stability

#### Tumor Boss
- punishes rigidity
- rewards adaptation

#### Chorus Boss
- punishes isolation
- rewards synchronization

#### Penitent Boss
- punishes hesitation
- rewards sacrifice

---

## 10. Living World Simulation (Interpretation System)

The game simulates “aliveness” via hidden systems:

### Hidden Doctrine Tracking
- Sanctum score
- Tumor score
- Chorus score
- Penitent score

### Key Rule

> The world reacts with delay, not immediacy.

This creates the illusion of memory and intent.

---

## 11. Room Generation Philosophy

Dungeon layout reflects doctrine:

### Sanctum Rooms
- geometric
- symmetrical
- predictable

### Tumor Rooms
- organic
- evolving mid-run
- asymmetric

### Chorus Rooms
- mirrored layouts
- synchronized hazards
- cooperative patterns

### Penitent Rooms
- sparse
- oppressive silence
- delayed threats

---

## 12. Relic Adjacency = Theology Syntax

Relic placement forms “sentences of belief.”

### Patterns

- Linear chains → Sanctum logic
- Web clusters → Tumor logic
- Mirror pairs → Chorus logic
- Isolation nodes → Penitent logic

> Players unknowingly write ideology through builds.

---

## 13. Mutations (Future Expansion System)

Players physically evolve during runs:

- visual sprite changes
- mechanical alterations
- identity shifts

### Concept

> Strength is not numeric — it is morphological.

Examples:
- bone growth increases melee range
- multiple eyes improve vision
- tumors increase instability but power
- parasitic symbiosis alters relic behavior

---

## 14. Core Narrative Problem

Why descend?

### Answer:

> Reality is destabilizing due to conflicting interpretations of existence.

The “Pulse” (reality engine) is fading.

Without intervention:

- existence collapses into silence

---

## 15. The Ending Structure

There is no single ending.

Instead:

### Layered Endings

- Run endings (escape, wipe, sacrifice)
- Zone endings (local truths)
- Cycle endings (meta progression milestones)
- Faction endings (interpretive conclusions)

### Key Rule

> Every ending is valid but incomplete.

---

## 16. Core Psychological Hook

Players do not choose classes.

They reveal beliefs under pressure.

> Build = Doctrine  
> Doctrine = Behavior under stress  
> Behavior = World interpretation

---

## 17. Core Design Identity

Veins is:

> A co-op roguelike where reality interprets player ideology and responds in kind.

---

## 18. Final Core Statement

> The game does not contain a world.
> It contains a system that decides what the world is based on what players consistently believe while playing.
